Day 1, Cairo Optional Pyramids Tour (Wednesday)
  • You have the day free at your leisure OR you may choose our Optional Pyramids Tour starts with a visit to the one and only standing monument in the seven wonders of the ancient world, The Great Pyramid of Khufu. In front of the glorious building, you will know and see why the Great Pyramid was chosen to be the most famous monument in the whole world. Carrying on to Giza plateau, you will see The Pyramids of Khafra, Menkaura, the well-preserved valley temple of Khaf and the Great Sphinx guarding the area.

  • The next stop will be a nice restaurant for having lunch at Giza. Our tour will continue to Memphis, south west of Giza pyramids, the 1st capital of Ancient Egypt. The ancient city hosts a number of breathtaking monuments such as: the colossi of Ramases II, the alabaster sphinx and other statues

  • The last stop of our tour will be The Pyramid Complex of Sakkara. It is interesting to see the evolution of pyramids construction and to witness the first step pyramid built by king Djoser of old kingdom

  • Meals served: Breakfast.
    • Day 2, Cairo Optional City Tours, Sleeper Train to Aswan (Thursday)
  • You have the day free at your leisure OR you may choose our Optional Cairo City Tour. The first stop will be the famous bazaar or market Khan El-Khalili in the historic center of Cairo. The ancient market is as old as the 14th century, and is characterized by the manufacture of a unique handicrafts and souvenirs made by the Egyptian talented artisans.

  • Constructed by the legendary army leader Saladin in the 13th century, The Citadel of Saladin will be our second stop. The citadel area encompasses several museums and monuments from different eras such as: the great alabaster Mosque of Mohamed Ali, the Royal Carriage Museum and Al-Gawhara Palace Museum

  • Afterwards, lunch will be served in one of the best local cuisine restaurants in Cairo.

  • We proceed to discover Cairo by visiting the area of Old Cairo, also known as the "Center of Religions" where a lot of historical churches, monuments, Ben Ezra synagogue and Amr Ibn El-Aas Mosque are found.

  • Our last stop of the day will be the Egyptian Museum in El-Tahrir Square, the home of Egypt's most valuable antiquities. You will be amazed by the number of treasures gathered in this building. It is highly recommended that you access "the Royal Mummy Room", at extra fees, to have a glimpse of the mummified great kings and queens of Ancient Egypt!

  • You will then go to Giza Railway Station from the hotel, to catch the overnight sleeper train heading to Aswan.

  • Meals served: Breakfast and Dinner
    • Day 3, Arrival to Aswan, Optional Sightseeing Tour (Friday)
  • Arrive to Aswan in the morning; you will then reach the luxuries Nile Cruise where you will spend the next three nights to start your relaxing tour sailing from Aswan to Luxor.
  • After having lunch, you have the day free at your leisure or you may choose our Optional Nile Cruise Sightseeing Tour.
  • Starting our tour by visiting The Aswan High Dam built across the Nile between 1960 and 1970. The importance of The High Dam lies in controlling the annual flood, providing increased storage of water used for irrigation and generating hydroelectricity. It has significant influence on the Egyptian economy and culture

  • The last stop of the day is Philae Temple. The whole temple was dismantled and reassembled within the UNESO project to save it from the annual Nile flooding. The ancient temple was dedicated to the fertility goddess Isis and it has artifacts, scenes and statues from different history layers.

  • At night you may choose the Optional breathtaking Sound and Light show at Philae Temple.

  • Meals served: Breakfast on the train, Lunch and Dinner
    • Day 4, Aswan, Kom Ombo, Edfu and An Optional Sightseeing Tour (Saturday)
  • We will sail to Kom Ombo at the early morning and visit the Temple of Kom Ombo as a part of our Optional Nile Cruise Sightseeing Tour. This temple is an unusual double temple built in the Ptolemaic dynasty of Egypt. It is recognized by the symmetrical interior design dedicated to different gods and goddesses

  • Sail to Edfu for having dinner

  • Meals served: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner.
    • Day 5, Edfu, Luxor, Optional Sightseeing Tour (Sunday)
  • Arrive at Edfu and start our Optional Sightseeing Tour to the Temple of Edfu, one of the best preserved temples in Egypt. It was built during the Ptolemaic dynasty dedicated to the falcon god Horus. It provides important information about religion, myth and language during the Greco-Roman Dynasty in Ancient Egypt.

  • We will then be back to the Nile Cruise to have lunch while heading to Luxor.

  • In the afternoon, you will visit the outstanding Karnak Temple as part of our Optional Tour. The largest cult temple ever built in Ancient Egypt, dedicated to the Theban triad Amun, Mut and Khonsou. It is a city of temples built upon a combined effort of many great kings and queens. You should never miss catching a glimpse to the glory of Ancient Egypt represented in Karnak Temple!

  • Temple of Luxor will be our next stop. It is built by the two great pharaohs Amenhotep III and Ramases II. The temple is easily recognized by a huge obelisk and two colossal statues flanking the entrance. Luxor temple was dedicated to the famous sun god of Ancient Egypt, Amun Ra.

  • Evening, you may choose the Light and Sound Show inside Karnak Temple as part of our Optional Sightseeing Tour.

  • Meals Served: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner.
    • Day 6, Luxor, Sleeper Train heading to Cairo (Monday)
  • Breakfast will be served on the Nile Cruise, and then we will proceed to the checkout. You will then have the rest of the day at your leisure with lunch at local restaurant. In the afternoon, you will go to Luxor Railway Station in order to catch the overnight sleeper train to Cairo

  • Meals served: Breakfast, Lunch and Dinner on the train.
    • Day 7, Cairo, Optional Full Day Tour to Alexandria (Tuesday)
  • Breakfast will be served on train, after which you will arrive to Cairo and to your Hotel. Afterwards, you have the day free at your leisure or you may choose our Optional Tour to Alexandria by private car. Alexandria is a charming costal city on the Mediterranean Sea. You will be accompanied by our professional tour guide among number of touristic attractions such as: Bibliotheca Alexandrina, the Cata Comp of Kom El-Shoqafa and the National Museum.

  • Return to Cairo.

  • Meals served: Breakfast.
    • Day 8, Departure (Wednesday)
  • Transfer to Cairo International Airport for Departure after having breakfast at the hotel

  • Meals served: Breakfast.
